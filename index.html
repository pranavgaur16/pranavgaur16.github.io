How I think

Product philosophy.

A few things I believe deeply after years of shipping AI products.

01
Complexity is a product decision.

Every piece of complexity you ship is a permanent tax on every user. I hold the line on simplicity — especially in AI, where systems can spiral fast.

02
A model without measurement is expensive guesswork.

I define quality metrics and experimentation frameworks before writing a line of product spec. Data is only useful if you designed to collect it intentionally.

03
The best AI is invisible to the user.

The greatest AI achievement isn't the technology — it's when users never feel it. That's the bar I hold my products to: powerful, invisible, inevitable.

04
Speed is a feature. So is restraint.

I've killed projects I loved because the data said no. Knowing when not to ship is the most underrated PM skill, especially when hype can override judgment.
